Gap Global Design System

2020-2021

The Gap CMS project occured during my time at Bounteous and our engagement with Gap Inc. At the time, Gap was transitioning to a digital-first approach. This move involved transitioning from an in-house web content development platform to Amplience, a headless content management system. This change was aimed at enhancing content management efficiency across its range of brands, including Old Navy, Athleta, Gap, Gap Factory, Banana Republic, and Banana Republic Factory.


Team Members & Collaborators

Bounteous and Gap Development Teams, Bounteous and Gap Design Teams, Product Management, Marketing Stakeholders

Tools

Photoshop, Adobe XD, Amplience, Excel

The goal for this project was to adopt a leading cms to enhance digital engagement across multiple channels including responsive web, mobile, and email. Ideally, this cms would empower non-technical users within Gap's brands to independently manage content, accelerating their workflows and streamlining the content localization, translation, and personalization processes.

However, integrating a cms across brands posed challenges, especially since each brand previously operated independently without a unified approach to design or component functionality. The first challenge was that the creative teams behind the brands needed to envision their designs as modular components within the CMS. Before, each brand had been a silo, with little exchange on modular design or component functionality. This siloed approach was a roadblock to creating a cohesive, efficient component library.
Another challenge was content reusability & innovation. The ability to adapt and repurpose components across the ecosystem was pivotal. Without this flexibility, the brands risked constructing a bulky library of components that looked similar yet performed differently, which would have accrued a hefty amount of technical and design debt.


To address this, Bounteous proposed a global design system allowing shared components across brands, maintaining brand uniqueness while reducing the component library size and ensuring consistency in user interaction and accessibility. This system was planned to evolve with Gap's digital needs and included a workflow for managing and updating the component library.



As product owner, my role involved aligning business stakeholders within Gap and the Bounteous and Gap creative and development teams to ensure that both the designs and components satisfied business requirements and matched the brand's expectations within the global design system framework. Some of my responsibilities included conducting interviews with stakeholders, annotating requirements, and supplying documentation and specifications to facilitate the development team's work on building these components.

The new Hertz Mobile App achieved a 4.8 out 5 star rating on the App Store, compared to the previous 3.8 stars on the old app. The redesign also led to a 32% increase in monthly mobile app users and a 50% increase in sessions YOY. We also saw a positive impact in engagement, increasing screens/session by 84%.


Since launch, the mobile app team has continued to iterate and add new functionality. As a content team we were able to swap out marketing banners as new promotions arose and update special offers in the deals section. The new app had a lot more flexibility and enabled marketing to make direct updates- reducing time and expenses associated with developer involvement.

I was grateful to be a part of the mobile app development project- from updating string files to differentiating designs for both operating systems. I also got a sense of what goes into approvals and the best way to report updates to leadership.
